Introducing PneumoScan.

PneumoScan is an AI-powered tool that uses deep learning to analyze chest X-rays and detect signs of pneumonia. Trained on a dataset of 5,863 images, it can distinguish between normal and pneumonia-affected lungs in seconds.

How It Works

This tool uses a convolutional neural network (CNN) to detect pneumonia from chest X-ray images. It’s trained on a dataset of 5,863 images, split into two categories: NORMAL and PNEUMONIA. Images are resized to 64x64 pixels and normalized (pixel values scaled between 0 and 1). Data augmentation such as rotation, zoom, and horizontal flipping is applied to the training set to improve generalization. The model architecture consists of two convolutional layers with ReLU activation and max pooling, followed by a flattening layer, a dense hidden layer with 128 units, and a dropout layer to reduce overfitting. The final layer uses a softmax activation function to classify inputs into one of the two categories. The model is compiled using the Adam optimizer and trained for 10 epochs using categorical cross-entropy loss. After training, the model is saved and used in a Flask web app. In the app, users can upload a chest X-ray image, which is resized to 64x64 pixels and preprocessed just like the training data. The model predicts class probabilities using model.predict, and the most likely class (NORMAL or PNEUMONIA) is returned along with the confidence percentage. The result is then displayed on the webpage alongside the uploaded image.

Why This?

Pneumonia is a serious and potentially life-threatening respiratory condition that affects millions of people worldwide, especially children and the elderly. Early and accurate diagnosis is critical for effective treatment, yet in many areas, access to trained radiologists is limited and traditional diagnosis through X-ray interpretation can be time-consuming and prone to human error. This project leverages machine learning to assist in diagnosing pneumonia by automatically analyzing chest X-ray images. By training a neural network on thousands of labeled images, the system can detect subtle patterns that may be missed by the human eye, offering faster, more consistent, and more accessible diagnostics. Integrating AI into medical imaging workflows not only enhances accuracy but also helps reduce the burden on healthcare professionals, particularly in under-resourced or high-volume environments. The use of machine learning in healthcare has the potential to save lives, improve patient outcomes, and reduce healthcare costs, making it a vital area of development in the medical field.
